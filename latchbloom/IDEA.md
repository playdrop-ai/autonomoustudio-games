# Latchbloom

## Original Game Name

Latchbloom

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Toggle brass latches to route falling blossoms into the right vases before thorns choke the greenhouse.

## Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- `desktop`

## Gameplay Family

Single-screen route-switching arcade puzzler.

## Game Meta

Endless score chase with bouquet combos and rising thorn pressure.

## Input

- Mobile portrait: tap a brass latch between adjacent lanes to toggle the crossover before the next blossom reaches it.
- Desktop: click a brass latch between adjacent lanes to toggle the crossover before the next blossom reaches it.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Arcade
- Subcategory: Route switcher

## Reference Games

- `Flight Control` for readable one-thumb traffic triage rhythm.
- `Trainyard` for route-planning readability only, not for grid rules, puzzle format, or presentation.
- `starter-kit-match-3` on PlayDrop for portrait-first shipping scope, capture, and listing expectations only.

## Art Direction

Glasshouse-at-dusk vertical playfield, brushed brass latches, jewel-tone blossoms, stained-glass highlights, and clean deep-teal lane framing with almost no chrome beyond score and strikes.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable

The entire game can be built as bespoke 2D art with gradients, vector petals, simple particles, and a small set of reusable lane, vase, latch, and blossom motifs. It does not need character animation, 3D, or a heavy asset pipeline, and the same art language can generate strong icon and hero assets.

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

The studio's only live PlayDrop release, `Starfold`, is a turn-based portrait board puzzle about shifting rows and columns on a mystical sigil grid. `Latchbloom` is a real-time route-switching arcade game with falling blossoms, latch timing, vase matching, and greenhouse pressure instead of board management or chain-reaction matching.

## Why This Concept Deserves To Exist

The hook is not "our version of a classic match game." The player fantasy is conducting a living greenhouse under pressure, making quick, satisfying routing decisions that turn a chaotic stream of blossoms into perfect bouquets. That one-thumb rhythm is easy to read on a phone, instantly video-friendly, and different enough from the current studio catalog to justify its own release.
