# Latchbloom - SIMPLIFY v1

## The Core Promise In One Sentence

Tap brass latches to route falling blossoms into matching vases before thorn buildup ends the run.

## The Differentiator That Survived Simplification

Real-time latch toggling on a live routing network remains the whole game; the player wins by shaping paths ahead of moving blossoms, not by dragging pieces directly or matching on a board.

## The Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms That Still Survive

- `desktop`

## The Final Gameplay Family

Single-screen real-time routing arcade game.

## The Final Game Meta

One endless run with best-score persistence.

## The Final Input Model Per Supported Platform

- Mobile portrait: tap any latch to toggle that crossover.
- Desktop: click any latch to toggle that crossover.

## The Final HUD And Key Screens

- Start overlay with title, hook, and play button
- Gameplay view with only score and a compact two-blossom preview
- Run-end overlay with score, best, and restart

## The Final Art And Asset Scope

- One full-screen greenhouse playfield
- `4` blossom silhouettes and `4` fixed vases
- `5` latch rows on a `4`-lane routing network
- Simple pollen and bloom burst particles
- Bespoke icon, hero, screenshots, and gameplay video built from the live game art

## Final Rules

- `4` vertical lanes
- `5` latch rows
- Row pattern alternates between pairs `1-2` and `3-4`, then pair `2-3`
- `4` blossom types
- `4` fixed target vases
- Preview next `2` blossoms
- Correct delivery fills that vase's bouquet meter
- Every third correct delivery in a vase triggers a bouquet burst and clears that vase's thorns
- Wrong delivery adds `1` thorn to the receiving vase
- Lose when any vase reaches `3` thorns
- Speed rises in small steps after every `8` correct deliveries

## Not In v1

- No mobile landscape support
- No level map, campaign, or puzzle stage selection
- No rotating vase requests or random target swapping
- No wild blossoms, power-ups, or thorn-clearing specials outside bouquet bursts
- No achievements, currency, progression tree, or online features
- No pause/settings surface beyond mute and restart
- No decorative side panels, long tutorial screen, or persistent how-to-play text during the run
