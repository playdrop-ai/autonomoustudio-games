# Drifthook

## Original Game Name

Drifthook

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Drop a lantern lure through a moonlit lake, reel up the target fish in order, and keep your line intact as the water grows faster and more dangerous.

## Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- `desktop`

## Gameplay Family

Single-screen fishing timing arcade.

## Game Meta

Endless score chase built around rotating catch orders, line-health pressure, and rising night-water difficulty.

## Input

- Mobile portrait: press and hold anywhere in the playfield to lower the lantern lure, then release to reel it back up.
- Desktop: hold `Space` or the mouse button to lower the lure, then release to reel it back up.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Arcade
- Subcategory: Fishing order chase

## Reference Games

- `Ridiculous Fishing` for readable vertical-water anticipation only, not for launch spectacle, fish stacking, or comedy.
- `Desert Golfing` for patient one-touch rhythm and low-chrome mood only.
- `playdrop/app/pool-game` for restrained, honest presentation and a realistic PlayDrop quality bar.
- `kenneynl/asset-pack/fish-pack` and `kenneynl/asset-pack/nature-kit` for proof that a lake-and-fish art path is realistic if custom vector work needs help.

## Art Direction

Moonlit portrait lake cross-section with indigo-to-teal depth gradients, warm lantern gold around the lure, soft reed silhouettes at the surface, bright fish silhouettes with subtle inner glow, drifting bubbles, and almost no chrome beyond score, line knots, and the three-slot catch order strip.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable

The shipped look can be built from custom 2D vector silhouettes, gradients, masks, particles, and shader-lightweight glow rather than a heavy 3D asset pipeline. The fish and nature packs already prove the theme is easy to support if a few silhouettes or background props need backup, and the listing art can reuse the same lake, lure, and fish language without inventing a second visual system.

## Why This Concept Supports A Real Player Session

The run is endless, but it is not just "catch anything forever." The player is constantly reading a three-fish order card, deciding when to drop, and protecting four line knots while fish speeds, depth mixes, and snag density rise in phases. That creates a real 2-to-5-minute mastery target instead of a novelty that is fully understood in one clear.

## What Keeps Players Engaged After The First Good Run

- rotating three-fish order cards force target selection instead of passive catching
- distinct fish depth bands and movement patterns create repeatable mastery
- perfect-catch timing and order-completion bonuses reward precision
- completed orders repair the line, so strong play extends the run materially
- seeded phase ramps push toward longer score chases and better expert survival

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

`Starfold` is a turn-based line-shift puzzle, `Latchbloom` is a real-time route-switcher, and `Keyfall` is a rhythm runway. `Drifthook` is none of those. Its fantasy is quiet night fishing under pressure, and its interaction is depth timing plus target-order reading rather than board manipulation, routing, or beat matching.

## Why This Concept Deserves To Exist

The idea is not "our version of a fishing classic." The player-facing hook is the tension between calm moonlit presentation and precise order-driven catching: lower at the right moment, reel through the right silhouette, and stay alive long enough to turn a fragile line into a long streak. The order card, line-repair economy, and low-chrome nocturne look give it a clear identity beyond a theme swap.
