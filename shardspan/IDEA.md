# Shardspan

## Original Game Name

Shardspan

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Flip ancient beacons to make new sky bridges real and sprint the shattered relay before dusk erases your path.

## Best Platform

`desktop`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- None in v1.

## Gameplay Family

Single-course 3D phase-switch platformer.

## Game Meta

Short replayable time-attack relay with instant retry and best-run chasing.

## Input

- Desktop: `WASD` or arrow keys to move, mouse drag to orbit the camera when needed, `space` to jump.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Action
- Subcategory: 3D platformer

## Reference Games

- `playdrop/app/starter-kit-3d-platformer` for bundled low-poly movement baseline and realistic PlayDrop shipping scope.
- `Super Mario Galaxy` for floating-island readability and forward-moving jump cadence only, not for stars, planets, or structure.
- `Neon White` for restart-driven time-attack clarity and route-improvement energy only, not for combat or card systems.

## Art Direction

Floating sandstone shards above a storm-dark void, with amber and cobalt bridge pieces that blink between dormant and solid states when a beacon fires. The sky should read as late dusk: warm light on stone edges, deep indigo shadows underneath, and clean luminous beacon columns that make the next goal readable from a distance.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable

The public starter kit already ships the exact kind of low-poly floating-platform assets, character scale, jump arc, skybox treatment, and bundled audio needed for a strong v1. The custom work is mostly layout, recolor, lighting, simple VFX pulses, and a small number of bespoke beacon markers rather than a new character or full asset pipeline.

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

The studio's current PlayDrop catalog, `Starfold` and `Latchbloom`, is portrait-first 2D puzzle and routing work. `Shardspan` is desktop-first 3D movement gameplay with camera control, jump timing, checkpoint routing, and world-state switching instead of board manipulation, matching, or tap-lane management.

## Why This Concept Deserves To Exist

This is not "our version of a platformer demo." The fantasy is racing across a broken sky path while you actively rewire the course under your feet. The phase-switching beacons turn each jump into both an execution test and a route decision, which gives the release a new input feel, a clear visual hook for screenshots and video, and useful portfolio variation away from the studio's current portrait puzzle lane.
