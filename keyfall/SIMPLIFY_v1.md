# Keyfall - SIMPLIFY v1

## The Core Promise In One Sentence

Tap, hold, and chord clean glowing keys on a 3D runway before your four life pips run out.

## The Differentiator That Survived Simplification

The game still lives on a forward 3D runway, still scores timing accuracy, and still uses chords and holds instead of only single taps, so the v1 is not just a flat Piano Tiles reskin with fewer features.

## The Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms That Still Survive

- `desktop`

## The Final Gameplay Family

Endless rhythm runner.

## The Final Game Meta

One endless score run with best-score and longest-combo persistence.

## The Final Input Model Per Supported Platform

- Mobile portrait: tap one of four bottom hit lanes, hold long notes, two-finger press for chords.
- Desktop: `D`, `F`, `J`, `K` for the four lanes and `Space` to start or retry.

## The Final HUD And Key Screens

- Start overlay with title and `Play`
- Gameplay with only score, combo, four life pips, and a compact next-3-beat preview
- Game-over overlay with score, best, combo peak, and `Retry`

## The Final Art And Asset Scope

- One fixed camera and one runway scene
- Four lane slabs, one strike line, one tunnel backdrop, and one phrase gate effect
- Three note meshes: tap, hold, chord
- Four generated music tracks
- Five short Web Audio SFX
- Bespoke hero, icon, screenshots, and gameplay capture built from the shipped scene

## Final Rules

- `4` lanes
- `3` note types: tap, hold, chord
- `4` life pips
- `perfect` and `good` timing windows with miss outside them
- combo multiplier capped at `x5`
- phrase length `16` beats
- speed up every `2` phrases
- rotate through `4` generated tracks on phrase boundaries
- lose at `0` life

## Not In v1

- No mobile landscape mode
- No song select screen or custom playlists
- No more than `4` shipped music tracks
- No unlock tree, achievements, leaderboards, or online features
- No lyric syncing or pitch-specific note charts
- No left-hand/right-hand control remapping UI
- No pause menu beyond mute and retry
- No decorative lore, tutorial wall, or long how-to-play copy on top of the gameplay scene
