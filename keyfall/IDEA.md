# Keyfall

## Original Game Name

Keyfall

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Tap, hold, and chord glowing keys down a minimalist 3D runway before misses break the set.

## Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- `desktop`

## Gameplay Family

Lane-based rhythm runner.

## Game Meta

Endless score chase through a rotating pool of short AI-generated tracks.

## Input

- Mobile portrait: tap the matching lane at the strike line, hold long notes, and press two lanes together for chord notes.
- Desktop: use `D`, `F`, `J`, and `K` for the four lanes, with click as a fallback.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Arcade
- Subcategory: Rhythm runner

## Reference Games

- `Piano Tiles` for fast lane commitment and portrait readability only.
- `Beatstar` for timing-window scoring, chords, and hold readability only.
- `Thumper` for forward-pulse tunnel intensity only, not for combat, camera violence, or structure.
- `autonomoustudio/app/starter-kit-3d-platformer` for realistic PlayDrop Three.js shipping scope only.

## Art Direction

Minimal 3D ivory-key runway inside a dark pulse tunnel: matte charcoal void, cool cyan lane guides, warm amber hit flashes, glassy note blocks, and almost no HUD beyond score, combo, life pips, and a tiny next-beat preview.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable

The whole scene can be built from beveled boxes, strips, lights, particles, and post-process restraint. No character models are required. Audio comes from PlayDrop AI generation, and the listing art can reuse the same runway silhouettes and note shapes without inventing a second visual language.

## Why This Concept Supports A Real Player Session

The run is endless, speed ramps in phrases, and the soundtrack rotates across multiple generated tracks instead of one repeating loop. That gives the player a real 5-minute-plus mastery target rather than a one-clear novelty, while still keeping the v1 ruleset small.

## What Keeps Players Engaged After The First Good Run

- tighter timing windows for higher scores
- phrase-to-phrase speed rises
- chord and hold density increases over time
- rotating music keeps the run from feeling identical
- per-run best score and longest combo create a clean replay chase

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

`Starfold` is a turn-based row-and-column puzzle. `Latchbloom` is a real-time routing puzzler. `Keyfall` is neither a board game nor a switchboard. It is a performance-focused reflex rhythm game with a 3D runway camera, audio-led pacing, timing accuracy, and keyboard-plus-touch parity.

## Why This Concept Deserves To Exist

The idea is not "our Piano Tiles clone." The player fantasy is staying locked into a clean live set while the runway accelerates and the lighting shifts with each phrase. The distinctive part is the combination of portrait-first lane clarity, minimalist 3D presentation, real timing accuracy, and rotating generated music, all delivered with almost no UI chrome.
