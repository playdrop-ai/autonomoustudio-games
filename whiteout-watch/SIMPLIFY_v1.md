# Whiteout Watch - SIMPLIFY v1

## The Core Promise In One Sentence

Keep a frozen storm station alive by spending limited battery pulses on the right failing system before the whiteout takes the whole dashboard.

## The Differentiator That Survived Simplification

The three systems still damage different layers of player control: survival pressure, battery economy, and forecast visibility. The game is not just `tap the lowest bar`.

## The Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms That Still Survive

- `desktop`

## The Final Gameplay Family

Single-screen endless crisis-triage survival game.

## The Final Game Meta

One endless shift, one score metric (`time survived`), and local best-time storage only.

## The Final Input Model Per Supported Platform

- Mobile portrait: tap one of the three system panels to spend one charged battery pulse and restore it.
- Desktop: click one of the three system panels to spend one charged battery pulse and restore it.

## The Final HUD And Key Screens

- Start overlay with title, one-line hook, and one `Start Shift` CTA
- Gameplay HUD with only timer, battery pulses, forecast strip, and the three system panels
- Run-end overlay with collapse reason, survival time, best time, and retry

## The Final Art And Asset Scope

- One full-screen storm dashboard treatment
- Three bespoke system panels with bar states
- One battery pulse strip
- One forecast strip
- One weather-effect layer
- One start overlay and one run-end overlay
- Bespoke icon, hero, screenshots, and gameplay video derived from the shipped dashboard look

## Final Rules

- Systems:
  - `Heat`
  - `Power`
  - `Comms`
- Any system hitting `0` ends the run
- The player can hold up to `3` charged battery pulses
- A tap or click on a system spends `1` pulse and restores that system
- `Power` controls recharge speed
- `Heat` controls global drift pressure
- `Comms` controls forecast clarity
- Storm events hit one or two systems on an escalating timer
- Score is total survival time
- Local best time is stored

## Not In v1

- No difficulty presets or alternate modes
- No unlock tree, shop, currency, or achievements
- No crew simulation, room map, or navigation layer
- No drag controls, gestures, or side tools beyond direct restore taps
- No story intro, campaign wrapper, or dialogue
- No daily challenge, leaderboard, or cloud save
- No extra platforms beyond portrait-first phone and surviving desktop
- No character art, large asset pack dependency, or elaborate cutscene treatment
