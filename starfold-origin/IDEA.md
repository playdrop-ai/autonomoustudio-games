# Starfold

## Original Game Name

Starfold

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Swipe whole rows and columns of glowing sigils to weave cascading constellations before ash consumes the shrine.

## Best Platform

`mobile portrait`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- `desktop`

## Gameplay Family

Single-screen line-swipe puzzle with chain reactions.

## Game Meta

Arcade score chase with rising board pressure from spreading ash blockers.

## Input

- Mobile portrait: swipe directly across a row or column to shift that line by one cell.
- Desktop: click-drag across a row or column to shift that line by one cell.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Puzzle
- Subcategory: Line shifter

## Reference Games

- `You Must Build A Boat` for whole-line manipulation
- `Two Dots` for satisfying chain-reaction readability
- `starter-kit-match-3` on PlayDrop for portrait board presentation and shipping scope only, not for rules

## Art Direction

Midnight shrine cloth, enamel-like sigils, ember glow, soft gold framing, and clean high-contrast cosmic gradients with no faux-mobile chrome.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable

The entire look can be built with custom vector shapes, gradients, particles, and typography inside a single TypeScript canvas app. No heavy 3D, character animation, or large asset pipeline is required, and bespoke listing art can be composed from the same sigil language.

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

The studio currently has no live PlayDrop game listing, and the previously deleted `pocket-2048` concept was a number-merge puzzle. `Starfold` is a whole-line swipe score-chase with moving ash blockers, a different fantasy, and a different interaction model.

## Why This Concept Deserves To Exist

The hook is not "our version of match-3." The player fantasy is weaving a living board of constellations by shifting the entire fabric of the shrine. Whole-line swipes create a faster, more tactile rhythm than tap-to-swap puzzlers, while the ash pressure turns every turn into board management instead of simple pattern hunting.
