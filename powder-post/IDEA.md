# Powder Post

## Original Game Name

Powder Post

## One-line Player-facing Value Proposition

Carve downhill through the right village gates, chain parcel deliveries, and stay ahead of the avalanche.

## Best Platform

`mobile landscape`

## Additional Supported Platforms

- `desktop`

## Gameplay Family

Landscape-first downhill slalom runner.

## Proven Reference Loop And Why Players Will Understand It Immediately

The reference loop is the classic downhill ski-dodge lane: steer left and right, miss trees and rocks, and survive as the slope speeds up. Players already understand this from `SkiFree`, `Alto`-style downhill games, and simple slalom toys. The screen reads instantly because the fantasy and controls match what the player expects from a skier on a mountain.

## Allowed Novelty Beyond The Reference Loop

The novelty is the delivery rule, not a new movement grammar. The courier carries a visible parcel crest, and only gates with the matching crest count as successful deliveries. That changes line choice and risk appetite without changing the primary interaction language of carving downhill and dodging hazards.

## Game Meta

Single endless score chase with parcel streaks, avalanche pressure, and best-score persistence.

## Input

- Mobile landscape: drag anywhere horizontally to carve left or right across the slope.
- Desktop: move with `A` / `D`, the arrow keys, or pointer drag.

## Game Category And Subcategory

- Category: Arcade
- Subcategory: Slalom runner

## Reference Games

- `SkiFree` for the proven downhill dodge loop.
- `Alto's Adventure` for readable downhill flow and scenic desire.
- `deryatr_/app/pixel_sky_hop` on PlayDrop for compact endless-run scope.
- `playdrop/app/starter-kit-racing` on PlayDrop for landscape action framing, capture expectations, and realistic release quality.

## Art Direction

Midnight-blue alpine slope, warm lantern gates, courier satchel crests, powder spray, dark pines, and a pale avalanche lip chasing from uphill. The game should feel brisk and cold in the environment but warm and inviting around the postal targets.

## Why The Art Direction Is Achievable With Available Assets, AI Generation, Or Simple Custom Work

The build can ship with a very small art set: one skier silhouette, a few gate crests, a handful of trees and rocks, a chalet-light language, and layered snow effects. It does not require character dialogue, dense animation, or a heavy 3D asset pipeline. The same visuals can produce strong screenshots, hero art, and icon direction later.

## Target Player And Why That Player Already Likes The Proven Loop

The target player likes quick reflex arcade games with clear risk-reward choices and repeatable score-chase sessions. That player already understands downhill survival and wants immediate movement flow rather than long explanation.

## Why The Fiction, Story Wrapper, And Marketing Promise Intensify The Same Player's Desire

The courier fantasy reinforces the same thing the action player already wants: threading tight lines under pressure. Delivering mail through the right glowing gates gives the slalom lane a more concrete purpose without asking for a different audience than the action loop itself.

## What The Player Is Trying To Uncover, Protect, Collect, Or Avoid

The player is trying to deliver the current parcel into the correct village gate, protect the streak, and avoid trees, rocks, and the avalanche front. The fail state makes intuitive sense because a courier who crashes or misses too many good lines gets swallowed by the storm.

## Why The Player's Main Verb Feels Desirable Instead Of Mean, Foolish, Or Self-defeating

Carving clean lines through warm gates while a storm chases behind feels skillful and heroic. The player is not doing something perverse or joyless; they are pulling off stylish, high-pressure saves on a dangerous slope.

## Why A Player Would Want This Instead Of The Best-known Game In The Same Lane

The best-known downhill dodgers usually ask only for survival. `Powder Post` adds a visible gate-choice layer that changes the first-minute decisions every few seconds. The player is not merely avoiding obstacles; they are choosing lines that preserve a delivery streak and buy space from the avalanche.

## The Brutally Honest One-line Description Of The Concept And Why It Does Not Collapse Into "Famous Game X With A Skin"

Brutally honest description: a `SkiFree`-style downhill slalom where you must thread the right delivery gates to keep the avalanche back.

That is still not just a ski skin-swap, because the matching-gate rule changes what the player is looking for and what counts as a good line from the first 10 seconds onward.

## Why The One- Or Two-sentence Pitch Creates Immediate Desire Instead Of Merely Sounding Coherent

The pitch promises speed, narrow escapes, and visible streak-building under pressure. A skier outrunning an avalanche while nailing parcel drops is immediately more click-worthy than a generic "snow game" or a sterile obstacle course.

## What Promise The Title, Icon, Hero, And First 15 Seconds Will Imply

The title, icon, hero, and first 15 seconds should all promise a stylish alpine courier fantasy: warm delivery targets cutting through cold mountain danger. The shipped gameplay will honestly fulfill that promise because the actual loop is about hitting those glowing gates at speed while the storm closes in.

## Why The Concept Supports A Real Player Session Instead Of A One-clear Toy

It is an endless score game with escalating speed, shifting obstacle density, and constant route pressure. A fresh player can survive for a short run, while a skilled player has enough room for a long mastery chase.

## What Keeps Players Engaged After The First Minute

Procedural gate layouts, improving streak management, faster avalanche pressure, and the pursuit of higher delivery chains keep the game alive after the first minute. The player always has a reason to take one more run and push a better route.

## Why The Game Is Distinct From The Studio's Existing Catalog

The studio catalog is currently heavy on portrait-first puzzle and arcade games built around row shifts, route toggles, card chaining, fruit clusters, and timing taps. `Powder Post` is a landscape action game with continuous steering, downhill motion, and an alpine courier fantasy instead of another low-chrome portrait puzzle.

## Starter, Demo, Template, Or Remix Path Distinctness

The project starts from `playdrop/template/typescript_template` only for SDK/bootstrap plumbing. That template has no visible gameplay signature. The first screenshot and first 15 seconds will belong entirely to the courier slope, gate crests, obstacle field, and avalanche framing.
